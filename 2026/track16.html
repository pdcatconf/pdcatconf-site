Introduction

The convergence of artificial intelligence, advanced sensing technologies, and clinical medicine is reshaping the landscape of modern healthcare, giving rise to a new interdisciplinary frontier—Medical-Engineering Integration (MEI). With the explosive growth of multi-modal medical data (e.g., imaging, genomics, electronic health records, and real-time physiological signals), there is an urgent need for intelligent computing paradigms that bridge engineering innovations with clinical practice. This track focuses on the synergistic application of AI-driven methodologies and engineering systems to address critical challenges in disease diagnosis, treatment planning, surgical robotics, remote patient monitoring, and personalized medicine. We invite cutting-edge contributions that span theoretical foundations, algorithmic developments, and deployable system implementations, with an emphasis on reliability, interpretability, and regulatory compliance in real-world healthcare environments. By fostering cross-disciplinary dialogue, this track aims to accelerate the translation of engineering breakthroughs into clinically actionable solutions that improve patient outcomes and healthcare delivery efficiency.

Topics

  • Deep Learning and Foundation Models for Multi-Modal Medical Data Analysis
  • Intelligent Surgical Robotics and Computer-Assisted Intervention Systems
  • Wearable Biosensors and AI-Driven Remote Patient Monitoring
  • Federated Learning and Privacy-Preserving Medical Data Sharing
  • Digital Twins and Simulation-Driven Personalized Treatment Planning
  • Edge-AI for Real-Time Clinical Decision Support and Emergency Response
  • Explainable AI (XAI) and Trustworthy Medical Diagnosis Systems
  • Medical Image Reconstruction, Enhancement, and Automated Interpretation
  • Rehabilitation Engineering and Brain-Computer Interface Technologies
